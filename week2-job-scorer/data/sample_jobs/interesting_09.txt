Data Engineer — Planetary Defense (NASA JPL)

Pasadena, CA — Onsite (US Citizenship Required)
GS-13/14 Equivalent: $120,000 - $160,000

The Jet Propulsion Laboratory, managed by Caltech for NASA, is seeking a Data Engineer to support the Planetary Defense Coordination Office. You'll work on the data systems that track near-Earth objects and assess potential impact threats.

About the Mission:
Every day, telescopes around the world scan the sky for asteroids and comets that could pose a threat to Earth. The data infrastructure you build will help process observations, compute orbits, and assess impact probabilities for thousands of near-Earth objects.

Responsibilities:
- Build data pipelines for astronomical observation processing
- Develop databases for orbital mechanics computations
- Create data integration systems connecting global telescope networks
- Maintain and improve the Near-Earth Object database
- Support mission planning data needs for planetary defense missions
- Build visualization and reporting tools for impact risk assessment

Requirements:
- 4+ years of data engineering or scientific computing experience
- Python programming proficiency
- SQL and relational database experience
- Experience with scientific data processing pipelines
- Understanding of data quality and validation
- US Citizenship required (security clearance may be needed)
- BS in Computer Science, Physics, Astronomy, or related field

Nice to Have:
- Astronomy or astrophysics background
- GCP or AWS cloud experience
- Experience with time-series or geospatial data
- Familiarity with FITS data format
